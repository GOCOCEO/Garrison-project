About

Challenge
Over Comfort.

The Garrison Project is built on one belief: boys are more capable than the system around them gives them credit for. The best thing you can do for a young man is challenge him - not protect him from difficulty.

The program is free. The work is not. Boys earn their place here through attendance, effort, and accountability. The mat teaches leverage - how to accept a bad position and work from it. How to stay composed when things are hard. How to lead when everyone around you is folding.

On top of the Jiu Jitsu, students work on practical life skills - getting an ID, filling out a job application, writing a resume, mock interviews, and college applications. None of this is abstract. All of it matters when they turn 18.

Founded by Angelo Coates and Eder Gomez. San Diego, California. A nonprofit initiative operating under GoCo Dynamics LLC.

Core Values
What We Stand On

Results,
Not Rhetoric.

The Garrison Project does not measure success by feelings expressed in a session. It measures success by what a young man actually does.

A grade going from D to B is proof of concept. Attendance is proof of commitment. A completed job application is proof of capability. That is the standard.

No funder, partner, or ideology rewrites the program methodology. The mission stays the mission.

Partner With Us
Proactive Over Reactive We build capacity before the crisis. Not after the diagnosis, not after the arrest. Before.
Challenge Over Comfort Growth happens on the mat, not in a circle. Boys need to be pushed, not protected from difficulty.
